## Concept

An octave is a 2:1 frequency ratio. A perfect fifth is 3:2. A tritone is roughly 45:32 — the famously unstable interval, the devil's chord.

If frequency relationships are ratios, and ratios are proportions, and proportions are spatial — what do musical intervals look like as printed objects?

Twelve posters. One for each interval in the chromatic scale. Each poster uses the interval's frequency ratio to set every proportion on the page: type size, column width, margin, leading, the gap between elements. The unison poster is perfectly symmetrical. The tritone is deeply uncomfortable.

## Process

Designed in Figma, output to Illustrator for print prep, printed on a Risograph RZ570 at BKLYN Print Lab in two-color runs — fluorescent orange and black for the consonant intervals, fluorescent pink and teal for the dissonant ones.

> The grid is always a musical score. This project just made it audible.
