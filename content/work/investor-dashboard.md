## Overview

The fund had grown from 8 to 43 portfolio companies in 18 months. Their existing reporting tool was a shared Google Sheet. The ask was simple: make it not that.

What emerged was less a dashboard and more an investment operating system — one surface where a GP could move from fund-level emissions roll-up to a single company's capital deployment timeline in three clicks.

## The constraint that made the design

Every stakeholder had a different definition of "performance." The CFO wanted IRR. The ESG lead wanted Scope 2 intensity. The GPs wanted momentum — which companies were accelerating, which were plateauing. 

> The insight was that these weren't competing views. They were the same data at different levels of zoom.

Rather than building separate tabs for each persona, we built a single canvas with a zoom model. Scroll out: fund-level aggregate. Scroll in: individual company detail. The hierarchy was spatial, not navigational.

## What we measured

Three signal types drove the entire visual language:

- **Financial performance** — IRR, TVPI, DPI, and runway relative to sector benchmarks
- **Climate metrics** — Scope 1/2/3 emissions, intensity ratios, year-over-year delta
- **Momentum indicators** — headcount growth, revenue growth, milestone completion rate

Each signal used the same visual encoding: a sparkline for trend, a badge for status, a number for the current value. Once you learned the pattern on one card, every card was readable.

## Outcome

Reduced weekly reporting prep time from ~6 hours to under 30 minutes. The ESG lead described it as "the first time I've been able to actually argue with the data instead of just presenting it."
